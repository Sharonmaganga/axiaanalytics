Part 1: What is AI Consulting?

Defining AI Consulting

AI consulting is strategic guidance from experts who help organizations identify, plan, and implement artificial intelligence solutions. Unlike software development firms that build specific applications, AI consultants take a holistic approach:

  • Strategic Assessment: Evaluate your organization's AI readiness and opportunities
  • Technology Selection: Recommend the right AI tools and platforms for your needs
  • Implementation Planning: Create a phased roadmap with clear milestones
  • Execution Support: Guide teams through the implementation process
  • Ongoing Optimization: Monitor performance and refine solutions

Think of it this way: a software developer builds the house, but an AI consultant helps you decide what kind of house you need, where to build it, and how to furnish it for maximum value.

Part 3: What AI Consultants Actually Do

1. AI Readiness Assessment

Before implementing AI, consultants evaluate whether your organization is ready. This includes:

  • Data availability and quality assessment
  • Current technology infrastructure evaluation
  • Team skills and capacity review
  • Business process analysis
  • Competitive landscape evaluation

A comprehensive AI readiness assessment typically costs KES 150,000-400,000 and takes 2-4 weeks.

2. Strategic Roadmapping

Consultants create a phased implementation plan that typically looks like:

  • Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Quick wins—low-hanging fruit with immediate ROI
  • Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Core initiatives—major transformation projects
  • Phase 3 (Months 7-12): Advanced capabilities—sophisticated AI systems

3. Technology Stack Selection

Consultants recommend the right mix of tools:

Category Options Best For
ML Frameworks TensorFlow, PyTorch, Scikit-learn Building custom models
Cloud Platforms AWS, Azure, GCP Scalability and infrastructure
BI Tools Power BI, Tableau, Looker Data visualization and insights
No-Code AI AutoML, Vertex AI, Azure ML Non-technical teams

4. Implementation Support

Consultants don't just hand over a plan—they guide implementation through:

  • Team training and upskilling
  • Code review and best practices
  • Infrastructure setup
  • Data pipeline development
  • Testing and validation
  • Deployment and monitoring

Part 4: How to Choose an AI Consultant in Nairobi

The 12-Point Evaluation Checklist

Experience & Credentials

✓ Question: How many AI projects have you completed? In which industries?

Red Flag: Vague answers, few completed projects, no case studies

Technical Depth

✓ Question: Can you explain your approach to feature engineering, model selection, and hyperparameter tuning?

Red Flag: Can't explain technical concepts clearly

Industry Knowledge

✓ Question: Do you have experience in our specific industry? What are the common use cases?

Red Flag: No industry-specific experience or references

Local Presence

✓ Question: Do you have a team in Nairobi? Can we meet in person?

Red Flag: Completely offshore with no local presence

Clear Pricing

✓ Question: What's your pricing model? Fixed price, hourly, or outcome-based?

Red Flag: Vague pricing or hidden costs

References & Reviews

✓ Question: Can we speak with 3+ recent clients?

Red Flag: Unwilling to provide references
